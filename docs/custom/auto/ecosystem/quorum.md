# Quorum

**Part of the official Quilibrium ecosystem.** Relation to the network: Built on Quilibrium (an app that runs on Quilibrium and needs the network to work).

**The world's first secure, peer-to-peer, end-to-end encrypted group messenger**, capable of handling extremely large groups with the bandwidth of a direct message. Free to use, no phone number, no payment.

Quorum is built around a simple idea: **stay connected, stay invisible**. Conversations are end-to-end encrypted, so only you and your intended recipients can read what's said. The message brokering protocol goes a step further and prevents linking senders to recipients, keeping even your contact list private. Onboarding takes three steps: create a passkey, pick a display name, and set a contact photo (which is only ever shared with people you talk to). If you have a QNS `@username`, you can tie it to your Quorum account so the same handle follows you across the rest of the ecosystem.

Where typical messengers route everything through one company's servers, Quorum runs **peer-to-peer on the libp2p stack** and works over TCP, QUIC, Websockets, or LoRa radio. That means a group can keep talking when the internet is down, when a country is blocking platforms, or when there is no cellular coverage. **There is no central server to unplug.**

**Quorum Spaces are self-moderated.** Your friend group, your gaming clan, your journalist circle, your religious community, your DAO, all set their own rules instead of inheriting them from a platform. Quorum sits at the intersection of the visual familiarity of mainstream community chat apps, strong E2EE, crypto-native features, and the resilience of a decentralized network.

For crypto users, Quorum ships built-in tools that run on the Quilibrium network: **wallet management with role-based access**, so only the right people can create keys or sign transactions, and **multi-signature setups for DAOs and groups**. Messages can optionally be made non-repudiable, signed by your user key so they're cryptographically verifiable rather than easily forged in a screenshot.

Quorum is **fully open source under AGPL**. With no central authority in control, anyone can run their own version, build custom features, design themes, or fork what they don't like. It is available today as a **web app**, a **native desktop app** via Electron, and a **mobile app for iOS and Android**, with messages, contacts, and settings syncing across every device through a shared protocol.
