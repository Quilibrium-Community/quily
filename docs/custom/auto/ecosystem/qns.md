# Quilibrium Names Service

**Part of the official Quilibrium ecosystem.** Relation to the network: Built on Quilibrium (an app that runs on Quilibrium and needs the network to work).

**Claim your `@username` on the Quilibrium network: a permanent, on-chain identity that follows you across every Quilibrium app.** QNS replaces long cryptographic addresses with readable names, the same way DNS replaced raw IP addresses for the web.

A QNS name does double duty. On the messaging side you get an **`@yourname` handle for Quorum**, so people can find and message you with a single recognisable identifier. On the web side you get the **`yourname.q` namespace**, which can point to decentralised websites, application endpoints, or anything else you want to resolve. You can also create subdomains: if you own `coffee.q`, you control every name under it (`shop.coffee.q`, `beans.coffee.q`), and nobody else can mint a subdomain in your namespace without your permission.

A practical caveat on the `.q` side: **regular browsers like Chrome and Firefox don't resolve `.q` addresses natively yet.** Reaching `.q` sites today takes a browser extension or proxy, similar to how `.onion` addresses work on Tor. Native support is on the roadmap as more Quilibrium-aware tools ship (Quorum is building its own browser integration), but for now think of `.q` as the forward-looking part of the system and `@yourname` on Quorum as the part that's fully usable today.

**No renewals. No annual fees. No central authority that can revoke it.** QNS is a one-time purchase, secured on-chain. This is the headline difference vs. traditional domains (which expire and can be seized by registrars) and vs. ENS (which charges renewal fees year after year).

**Compared to ENS specifically, QNS adds a few things that the Ethereum model can't really support:** richer data per record (Quilibrium can store sizeable payloads, Ethereum can't), built-in communication keys for secure messaging in Quorum, the ability to point at full websites and app resources instead of just wallet addresses, and cryptographic self-verification with no certificate-authority middleman.

**Privacy is on by default.** A QNS name is not linked to your wallet unless you explicitly add that link as public metadata. The only thing exposed at resolution time is your Quorum identity key, which is the key for messaging, not your financial keys. You stay anonymous unless you choose otherwise.

The site at names.quilibrium.com is the live registry: search availability and **register** a name, browse the **marketplace** to buy and sell existing ones, or bid in **auctions** for contested names. Every registration earns **QNS Points**, which unlock rewards and a voice in how the service evolves.
