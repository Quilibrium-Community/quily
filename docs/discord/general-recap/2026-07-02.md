# Community Recap — #general

**July 2, 2026** | 1:38 AM UTC – 8:06 AM UTC

## From Cassie

Cassie weighed in on Ethereum's technical challenges, noting that while ETH's technical debt is "not insurmountable," it requires changes the team is "basically allergic to." She outlined a potential path forward for Ethereum: remove the blob fee market, move to ZK RISC-V, and partition network state into shards—but acknowledged these are breaking changes the devs resist. On a lighter note, she confirmed Frames should be back this week.

## Mainnet & Bridge Discussion

alcohol asked whether reaching the .25 milestone would trigger mainnet launch and bridge opening. The conversation then shifted to price speculation (which we skip), though lewsid2.0 noted that private finance, private AI, and private socials are three "behemoths" that could drive demand. alcohol clarified that true market cap rivaling Ethereum would require more mainnet features from 2.2/2.3, but called the existing foundation "incredible."

## Ethereum Criticism

Lukennnnnn offered sharp criticism of Ethereum's L2 strategy, calling it a "significant technological compromise" that wasted years and squandered market funds. He alleged that early developers—including Vitalik Buterin—had already made too much money to persevere, and noted L2 tokens developed by Buterin's mother as evidence of conflicts of interest. Several community members responded with "luckily we have Cassie."

## Technical Architecture

When asked about Ethereum's technical debt, Cassie explained that KZG blobs are now used only for L2s, whereas proper scaling would require sharding—a feature removed from ETH's roadmap. Her analysis highlighted design choices she believes would strengthen Ethereum but that the project is unwilling to adopt.
