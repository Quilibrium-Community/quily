# Community Recap — #general

**July 8, 2026** | 12:01 AM UTC – 8:05 AM UTC

## Daily Recap – 2026-07-08

### 🔧 Tech Updates
- Mr.Franc updated the [shard explorer](https://shard.qstorage.quilibrium.com/) with more info. Cassie clarified that many `.24` nodes are sending bad shardframe proofs; these end up in frame data but are inert.
- Cassie deployed an archive update to reject useless shardframes from `.24` peers, aiming to clean up logs and dashboards.
- Two issues being tackled: unexpected bandwidth consumption (reported in the node runner TG) and cluster mode nodes unable to join. Non-cluster nodes appear to be working correctly.

### 📱 Quorum & App Store
- Cassie explained why Quorum isn’t yet on the App Store: it’s a social app with messaging, voice/video, and a wallet – each function has its own review burden. Apple imposes strict UGC rules (reporting, content filters). The team plans to resubmit after the 2.1 release, which has taken priority.
- A major UI overhaul (by miko) is waiting for the next QM update.

### 🏦 UTXO Model & Money Laundering
- Cassie highlighted that Quilibrium uses private UTXOs, not an account model, so receiving dust from a sanctioned source does not contaminate your entire balance. Additionally, QUIL tokens require an explicit accept action.
- The bridge to ETH will use separate MPC accounts to avoid “contaminated” funds, learning from earlier industry pitfalls.

### 🗳️ BonkDAO & DAO Lessons
- The BonkDAO was drained of ~$20M via a malicious governance proposal that required only 1% token ownership. Cassie noted that majority control is majority control – like a PE firm liquidating assets. The incident sparked discussion about DAO design and the importance of voter participation.

### 📢 Upcoming Podcast
- Cassie will appear on the EVMavericks podcast this Friday (July 11, 11 AM PDT) to discuss the Farcaster fork, Quorum, and Quilibrium.
