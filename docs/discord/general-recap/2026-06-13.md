# Community Recap — #general

**June 13, 2026** | 12:00 AM UTC – 8:38 AM UTC

## From Cassie
Cassie addressed the earlier outage, confirming it was a "plain old outage" and that services were back online with a plan to prevent recurrence. She noted the outage affected an archive, which may have impacted peer count recovery.

She announced she would push a new branch, **2.1.0.24**, primarily to bring nodes on Ubuntu 22 up to speed and to add protections against further halts—thanks to Rust archives now proactively shielding new shards. Cassie expressed optimism: *"I actually do think we're gonna be out of the woods this weekend."*

On Quorum Apex, she advised users to wait before purchasing, as the price would be lowered (it started too high). She also reflected on Quorum's significance, calling it *"arguably why all this happened in the first place,"* and praised the ongoing cross-pollination between web and mobile versions.

## Network Status & Eviction
Users discussed the earlier downtime (QNS and Quorum down). After recovery, the eviction countdown was debated: halt risk hit 0, but the outage stopped the 720-frame timer. Cassie confirmed halt risk is stable at 0, and eviction should proceed normally once frames resume. Mr.Franc's dashboard showed 86% of nodes on version .23, raising questions about how many .22 nodes were evicted.

## Quorum & Community Migration
Mr.Quil expressed excitement about using Quorum as his primary messenger, anticipating word-of-mouth adoption. User aoki suggested migrating both Discord and Telegram communities to Quorum Messenger once mainnet is stable and proven. Cassie agreed, but noted the Quorum community is still inactive. Tyger reported not receiving new messages in the app, highlighting current limitations.
