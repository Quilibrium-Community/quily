# Community Recap — #general

**June 12, 2026** | 1:00 AM UTC – 9:54 AM UTC

## Quilibrium Network Status & v0.24 Development

The general channel was focused on the ongoing network stabilization efforts after the recent halt. Users tracked the "halt risk" metric, which needs to remain at 0 for a continuous 720 frames (roughly 12 hours) to trigger eviction of offline nodes.

**Cassie [LEAD DEV]** clarified that while halt risk briefly hit 0, it "ticked up again at 7:30am PDT" (1:37 AM UTC). She confirmed that eviction requires the halt risk to stay at 0 for the full grace period — and that hasn't happened yet. She also explained that shards with no data never count toward halt risk, and that shards with halt risk are prioritized.

## v0.24 Release Details

The upcoming v0.24 release was a major topic. Cassie stated the fix for the glibc issue on Ubuntu 22 nodes is already done, but the release is being held up by "other minor bits and annoyances" (7:27 AM UTC). The plan is to get v0.24 out so Ubuntu 22 nodes can upgrade — many nodes running v0.22 crashed when attempting v0.23 due to glibc incompatibility. Cassie confirmed the patch will cover all platforms, not just Ubuntu.

## Network Recovery Progress

Users tracked frame counts closely. alcohol noted that halt risk had been 0 for "at least 371 frames" (7:39 AM UTC) and later estimated 494 frames by 9:19 AM UTC. OnkelEagle reported a bot tracking 678/720 frames at one point but later found a timing issue with the bot. The mood was cautiously optimistic, with many hoping the 720-frame threshold would be reached soon to begin evictions.

## Logo Discussion

A brief sidebar on branding: Mr.Franc shared a redesigned logo, and yogurt and Finn commented on the orientation and the small circle in the center. Finn also liked a "speech bubble" logo from a previous day.

## Quilscan Update

Quilscan (https://quilscan.com/) announced an update including improved macOS agent stability, custom port checks, active sorting on Shards page, fixes for qclient commands, and a refined online status metric (nodes considered online if they've sent activity within the last 6 hours).

## Other Notes

- Quil is not an EVM token, though a wrapped version exists.
- A new node operator (aws) reported no shard assignment after 10 hours — Cassie explained this is expected given changing allocations.
- Several users expressed concern about having enough replacement nodes after evictions, but others noted most nodes are already on v0.23.
