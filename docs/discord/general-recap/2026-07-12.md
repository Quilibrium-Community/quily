## Technical Discussion: PCDN and Quil's Networking
- **Otter** shared a detailed analysis of **PCDN (Peer-to-Peer CDN)** technology, noting its relevance to Quil's architecture. Key suggestions: observe mesh coverage per shard, improve NAT traversal, isolate consensus traffic from bulk sync, and provide auditable P2P fallback.
- **neon** added that Quil already resembles a PCDN model (archive nodes as CDN nodes, non-archive as edge nodes) and referenced **blossomsub** for localized message delivery.
- Cassie confirmed Quil uses the bottom three of Otter's listed libraries (go-libp2p, go-libp2p-pubsub, pion ICE) and that blossomsub is a fork of gossipsub.
- Links shared:
  - [libtorrent](https://github.com/arvidn/libtorrent) – piece scheduling, congestion control

  - [go-libp2p](https://github.com/libp2p/go-libp2p) – discovery, NAT traversal, relays
