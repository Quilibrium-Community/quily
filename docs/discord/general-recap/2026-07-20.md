# Community Recap — #general

**July 20, 2026** | 4:00 AM UTC – 8:25 AM UTC

## From Cassie

Cassie announced a significant `.25` branch update (not a version release) coming within the next 24 hours, including migration steps for archives. The update stems from an exhaustive performance analysis that identified key gaps, leading to a complete replacement of all non‑post‑quantum safe primitives:

- **BLS48‑581 (Signatures) → Falcon** – BLS aggregation could not scale with larger shard counts; Falcon dramatically outperforms it.
- **ED448 (Peer keys, mTLS) → Falcon / NTRU** – Peer validation was bottlenecking the network as new nodes spun up; Falcon and NTRU collapsed verification time and processor burden.
- **Verkle/KZG → Merkle** – From low seconds to low milliseconds in worst case.
- **Decaf448/Schnorr/Bulletproofs → BDLOP/M‑SIS/M‑LWE** – Privacy and token operations ported to PQC equivalents with significant speedup.
- **Removed hand‑rolled generic consensus logic** – Replaced with battle‑tested libraries, reducing time‑to‑confirmation from 1‑3s to sub‑second.

Cassie emphasized that the PQC readiness is a side effect of performance optimization, not the primary goal. She also pre‑committed three hashes as responses to anticipated FUD, inviting “left curvers” to start their engines.

She later clarified that the branch update will be pushed to the git branch, but **no signed binary release will happen within 24 hours** – the testing must be fully green first.
