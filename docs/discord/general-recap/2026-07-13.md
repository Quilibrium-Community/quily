# Community Recap — #general

**July 13, 2026** | 3:43 AM UTC – 8:37 AM UTC

## Frames Stalling and Progress

Cassie [LEAD DEV] reported at 3:43 UTC that frames were moving again, but neon noted large intervals between frames. Cassie explained this is due to a lagging archive catching up — which caused the original stall — and predicted that once successful, frame time would drop to 10 seconds. Later, frames stopped again at height 673922. Cassie confirmed all archives are online and the process is running, but the cause is unclear. Unlike a previous stall caused by randomness-induced timeouts, this time randomness isn't picking provers that time out.

neon observed that frames got stuck after the 936th epoch, and one of their nodes claimed 49 active shards but had an idle worker — a behavior reminiscent of a past bug. Cassie speculated that a griefer might be DDoS-ing the network every time progress is made.

## Potential Causes and Investigation

Cassie and neon discussed clock skew as a possible explanation for inconsistent frame deltas. Cassie noted that if the same prover consistently shows -1 to 2s deltas, that would confirm clock skew. neon reported that their buggy node eventually returned to normal by dropping all shards and rejoining. Cassie suggested that node might have synced against an archive that was behind, causing it to recognize an old world state.

Cassie highlighted that the sudden appearance of many new nodes after frames resumed could be a key factor. Initial RPC connection negotiation for new peers takes 0.2–0.5 seconds, and spanning that across hundreds of new nodes could exhaust throughput — whether intentional or not. She is adding metrics to identify which specific issues are occurring.

## From Cassie

Cassie provided several technical diagnostics: the lagging archive caused the stall; clock skew is a possible explanation for frame time anomalies; and the influx of new nodes may be overwhelming RPC initialization. She is now adding metrics to better diagnose the problems.
