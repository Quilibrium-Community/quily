# Quilibrium General Channel — 2026-06-14 Recap

## Network Status and .24 Testing

Discussion continued around the ongoing test of the .24 release. Hyperstax noted that warning shards are down to 4 and that evictions can still occur even while halt risk is at zero, but the .24 release should enable full network launch. Danske reported deploying 555 workers and seeing allocation happening. DanielsanDDVcrypto asked why evictions are not happening yet; later, Finn observed that peers total vs. peers online metrics are now much closer. Alcohol clarified that the improvement was due to a reboot of archive nodes, not actual evictions. The community expressed interest in updates from Cassie on eviction timing.

## Missing Worker IDs in qclient

neon🦦 raised a detailed issue: on some machines, qclient does not display all workers — entire ranges of worker IDs are missing and cannot be selected, even when attempting to manually add shards. The problems seem to have started after switching to a version compiled via Quilscan and deleting `store` and `worker-store` folders while retaining `config` and `keys`. Cassie investigated, explaining that proposals occur in batches and that the logic waits for a join to be confirmed before pinning a shard to a worker core. She identified that the missing workers should eventually appear as idle (`-1`), but is still investigating why they are not showing in the lineup. Cassie also noted that users running quilscan builds should be mindful of verifying that the compiled source is 1:1 with the official repo.

## Quilscan Build Verification Discussion

LaMat raised security concerns about the Quilscan agent binary: no reproducible builds, no independent verification of the released binary against the repo. They noted that v1.0.9 added signed updates, but the build pipeline remains a trust point. Quilscan responded by acknowledging the issue — a past release was accidentally built from a dirty directory — and committed to moving to public GitHub-based builds for transparency. They linked to the public Dev Node repository on GitHub: [quilscan-dev-node](https://github.com/quilscan-com/quilscan-dev-node), where full build artifacts are available for verification.
