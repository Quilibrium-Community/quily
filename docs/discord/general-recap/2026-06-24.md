# Quilibrium General Chat Recap — 2026-06-24

## Eviction Progress & Status

The day began with the eviction clock ticking — Tyger reported the system was at 39/360 frames before inactive provers would be booted, later updating to 170/360. Cassie confirmed that 736 unique provers were eligible for eviction, warning operators to ensure their own provers weren't on the list.

By mid-day, Cassie noted evictions appeared to have run but the explorer wasn't reflecting changes. After investigating, she identified an archive connectivity issue and deployed a fix. By 7:30 PM UTC, she confirmed: **"evictions succeeded"** — with the warning count dropping to 1.

## From Cassie

- **Evictions**: Ran successfully after an archive connectivity issue was resolved. A deeper instrumentation build helped identify why evictions weren't triggering.
- **Archive performance**: One archive was underperforming, causing frame timeouts when it was up for proposal turns. Lower priority than overall stability, but being investigated.
- **Node operator advice**: If you stopped running nodes, manually issue leaves for workers (especially if they have seniority). Set up a node with one worker using proper config/key files, switch to manual mode in TUI, and issue leaves for existing workers.
- **Stability**: Waiting to see allocations rebalance and stability hold before declaring mainnet stable.
- **Development languages**: Rust (directly in protocol), QCL/Golang (MPC compute apps), Balance (post-2.1 apps).

## Node Issues & Troubleshooting

Logdog1775 reported sync issues with a node stuck at frame 670940, showing "fresh sync commitment mismatch" and "shard_info refresh failed." Tyger suggested Starlink's CGNAT could be the culprit — dedicated IP ≠ public IP, and CGNAT can prevent incoming connections, leading to blacklisting.

Hyperstax was stuck at 674616 and later 675532. Cassie recommended wiping stores, noting sync commitment mismatches aren't unexpected as long as frames keep advancing.

## Security Discussion

After news of a $20M hack on Cardano (SecondFi), the community discussed hardware wallet best practices: using 3 hardware wallets with multisig, passphrases (25th word), and keeping seed phrases in separate bank vaults. Cassie clarified the 25th word is a passphrase, not a checksum, but warned using an actual word as password is still high risk.

## Quilscan Update

Quilscan launched a **Shard Eviction view** at https://quilscan.com/rings, showing at-risk provers, affected shards, eviction counts, and risk ratios per shard with color-coded status indicators.

## Miscellaneous

- Cassie noted that one of the most important anonymity features is the hardest to concisely explain.
- Frames experienced some stalling due to archive performance issues, which Cassie is addressing.
- The community dealt with scam messages being posted and quickly deleted by moderators.
